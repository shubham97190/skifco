# NGO / Trust Website Project Start Guide

This document is a practical starting guide for building an NGO / Trust website based on the provided objectives.  
The goal is to create a website that feels real, trustworthy, human-written, and aligned with charitable/public welfare work.

---

## 1. Project Purpose

Build a professional NGO / Trust website that clearly explains:

- Who the Trust is
- Why the Trust exists
- What social problems it wants to solve
- Which programs it will run
- Who will benefit from the programs
- How donors, volunteers, CSR partners, institutions, and communities can connect
- How the Trust will maintain transparency and public trust

The website content should be original. We can study other NGO websites for structure, tone, and program ideas, but we should not copy their wording, layout, claims, impact numbers, photos, or project names.

---

## 2. Main Website Objective

The website should present the Trust as a public welfare organization working across:

1. Education and literacy
2. Healthcare and medical relief
3. Skill development and livelihood promotion
4. Environment and sustainable development
5. Research, innovation, STEM, robotics, AI, and technology-based learning
6. Partnerships with government, CSR, NGOs, and institutions
7. Community development and social empowerment
8. Disaster relief, scholarships, elderly care, legal aid, culture, yoga, and public welfare

The tone should be simple, honest, and grounded.  
Avoid over-promising. Use phrases like “we aim to”, “we plan to”, “our focus will be”, and “the Trust intends to support” until real projects and impact data are available.

## 4. Important Content Rule

When reviewing the above websites:

- Do not copy-paste any paragraph.
- Do not copy exact program names unless they are generic terms.
- Do not use another NGO’s impact numbers.
- Do not use another NGO’s photos or graphics without permission.
- Do not claim completed work unless the Trust has actually done it.
- Use local context, real intentions, and simple human language.
- Write content in your own words based on the Trust deed objectives.
- Keep the language warm, clear, and believable.

Good content should sound like it came from the founders and team, not from a generic AI template.

Example writing style:

> We believe that education, health, livelihood, and dignity are connected. A child cannot learn well without nutrition, a family cannot grow without health support, and a young person cannot become independent without practical skills. Our Trust has been formed to support these needs through community-focused programs, partnerships, and long-term public welfare initiatives.

## 9. Content Writing Style Guide

### Use This Tone

- Simple
- Human
- Respectful
- Grounded
- Honest
- Public welfare focused
- Trustworthy
- Community-oriented

### Avoid This Tone

- Too promotional
- Too emotional without facts
- Too corporate
- Too legal-heavy
- Overly AI-like
- Overpromising
- Claiming impact without proof

### Good Words To Use

- support
- empower
- enable
- serve
- guide
- collaborate
- improve access
- promote dignity
- create opportunities
- community participation
- public welfare
- inclusion
- transparency
- sustainable change

### Words To Avoid Overusing

- revolutionary
- world-class
- best NGO
- guaranteed impact
- life-changing for all
- massive impact
- unmatched
- biggest
- perfect solution

## 16. Technical Website Requirements

### Recommended Tech Stack

For a simple NGO website:

- Frontend: Next.js or React
- Styling: Tailwind CSS
- CMS option: Strapi, WordPress, Sanity, or simple admin panel
- Forms: Contact form, volunteer form, donation inquiry form
- Hosting: Vercel, Netlify, AWS, GCP, or shared hosting
- Database: PostgreSQL or MySQL, only if dynamic content is required
- Email: SMTP, SendGrid, AWS SES, or Zoho Mail
- Analytics: Google Analytics / Plausible
- SEO: Metadata, sitemap, robots.txt, schema markup
